Characters in Noli Me Tangere

Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin Crisostomo Ibarra is of Filipino-Spanish descent, and is the son of Don Rafael Ibarra, a wealthy Spaniard. Although he was born and raised in the Philippines, he spent seven years of his adolescence studying in Europe.
Maria Clara Maria Clara is the main female character in the novel. She is the daughter of Kapitan Tiago and Dona Pia Alba. While giving birth to Maria Clara, Dona Pia passed away. Therefore, Tiya Isabel, Kapit√°n Tiago's cousin, guided and oversaw Maria instead.
Padre Damaso Franciscan priest, Damaso Verdolagas, was the curate of the parish church of San Diego. He was curate for almost twenty years before Padre Salvi, who was considerably younger, took over.
Elias Elias came from a family that had been ruled by the Ibarra family for many years. He grew up raised in a decent home until he discovered something that ruined his life for good. Although Ibarra's relatives mistreated him, he owes them nothing.
Dona Victorina Dona Victorina de los Reyes de Espada was a woman who claimed to be a mestiza and dreamed of marrying a Spaniard. Everyone in the village feared her due to her unusual appearance, harsh personality, and fierce rivalry with Dona Consolacion.
